Got it. Clean and simple — capture without commitment, archive when done with it.Here are the fundamental requirements for the Someday List, rooted in GTD's core principle: **get it out of your head, without it becoming a commitment.**The 4 fundamental requirements:

**① Zero-friction capture** — Adding an item must be one tap, one field (title). The moment you add friction (forcing a tag, a category, a date), the brain resists capturing — and the idea dies unrecorded. GTD calls this the "trusted system" — it only works if capturing feels effortless.

**② No commitment fields** — No due date, no priority, no status. The psychological weight of those fields is exactly what Someday/Maybe is designed to remove. An item in Someday carries no obligation, which is the whole point.

**③ Weekly review nudge** — GTD is explicit: the Someday list is *only useful if you revisit it*. The app should prompt a periodic review — not auto-act, not nag — just surface the list and let the user decide each item's fate consciously.

**④ Two and only two exit paths** — Keep it (stays in Someday) or Archive/Delete it. No promoting to active tasks since you said that's out of scope. Keeping the exits minimal respects the GTD philosophy: the list should never feel like a to-do pile waiting to explode into your active system.
